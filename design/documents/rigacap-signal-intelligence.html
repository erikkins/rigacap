3. The Ensemble Signal — Three-Factor Entry

A stock must satisfy all three independent factors simultaneously before the system generates a buy signal. This multi-confirmation approach dramatically reduces false signals and ensures only the highest-conviction setups enter the portfolio.

Factor 1: DWAP Timing

The Daily Weighted Average Price (DWAP) captures the average cost basis of all traders over 200 trading days, weighted by volume. When price breaks above DWAP by 5%, it signals that the stock has cleared its accumulation zone with conviction.

DWAP = Σ(price × volume)200d / Σ(volume)200d
BUY when price > DWAP × 1.05

Factor 2: Momentum Quality

Stocks passing the DWAP filter are ranked by a composite momentum score that rewards short-term acceleration and sustained strength, while penalizing excessive volatility:

composite = short_mom10d × 0.5 + long_mom60d × 0.3 − volatility20d × 0.2

Transition Forecasting

Beyond static classification, the regime engine computes indicator trajectories to predict where the market is heading in the next 1–2 weeks:

  • VIX Delta (5-day): Rising VIX boosts bearish regime probabilities
  • Trend Acceleration: Compares recent 5-day return vs prior 5-day return
  • 200MA Proximity: Within 2% of 200MA triggers higher transition uncertainty
  • Sharp Moves: 5-day returns exceeding ±5% boost crash/recovery probabilities

The forecast produces a recommended action ranging from stay_invested → tighten_stops → reduce_exposure → go_to_cash.

Trailing Stop Mechanics

The trailing stop is calculated from the high water mark (HWM) — the highest price since entry, not the entry price itself:

HWM = max(entry_price, stored_highest, current_price)
stop_level = HWM × (1 − 0.12)

SELL when price ≤ stop_level     WARNING when distance < 3%

Exit Priority

  1. Regime Exit (go_to_cash): Immediate sell regardless of P&L — capital preservation
  2. Trailing Stop Hit: Price ≤ HWM × 0.88 — overrides regime warnings
  3. Warning Zone: Within 3% of stop level — email alert, no action
  4. Regime Warning: reduce_exposure or tighten_stops — email alert

Intraday DWAP Crossover Detection

The monitor also watches stocks on the dashboard watchlist (stocks approaching the DWAP threshold). If a watchlist stock crosses DWAP +5% intraday, the system:

  • Adds the stock to the dashboard as a fresh intraday signal
  • Removes it from the watchlist
  • Sends intraday signal alert emails to subscribed users (skipping users who already hold the stock)
  • Updates the S3 dashboard JSON in real-time

8. Walk-Forward Validation

Walk-forward testing is the gold standard for trading system validation. Unlike traditional backtesting which optimizes on all available data (introducing hindsight bias), walk-forward divides history into sequential periods and tests forward into data the optimizer has never seen.

Methodology

  • Period Length: Biweekly (14-day intervals, anchored Feb 1)
  • Optimization: Uses only historical data up to each period boundary
  • Trading: Enters fresh ensemble signals, force-closes at period end (rebalance_exit)
  • Position Carry: Carry-over outperforms force-close (A/B tested: +206% vs +121%)
  • Max Symbols: 100 per period for computational efficiency
  • Strategy: Ensemble (strategy_id: 5) — DWAP + Momentum + Regime

Zero Hindsight Bias: At each biweekly boundary, the system knows nothing about what happens next. Parameters are optimized on past data, then the system trades forward into unseen market conditions. The +497% return (10yr) is entirely out-of-sample.
