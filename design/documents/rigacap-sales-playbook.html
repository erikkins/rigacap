The Sales Playbook

How to find the investor this is right for — and disqualify the one it isn't. This product is unusual: the same engine is an A+ for one person and an F for another. Selling it well is an act of matching, not persuading.

The one rule that governs everything
Your job is filtration, not conversion.

Do not "close" everyone. A mis-sold investor is worse than a lost one — they churn in three months the moment the market rips and they feel they're "missing out," and they tell people it didn't work. A well-qualified investor stays for years, because you set the expectation honestly on day one. Disqualify fast, and disqualify out loud. The honest "this probably isn't for you" is your most powerful closing tool with the right person.

The honest one-liner (memorize this)

RigaCap is behavioral capital insurance — a regime-adaptive momentum engine that trades a little bull-market upside for dramatically smaller drawdowns and the discipline to stay invested when everyone else is panicking.

It is not a beat-the-market engine. It's a don't-blow-yourself-up engine. Lead with that. The people it fits will lean in; the people it doesn't will self-select out — which is exactly what you want.

The four people you'll meet

Sort every prospect into one of these in the first five minutes. Three you sell; the fourth (the Maximizer mindset) you politely release.

01  Preserver · flagship

The Capital Preserver

Who
55–70, $250k–$2M investable, at or near retirement — "I've made it; now I can't afford to lose it." Lived through 2008 and/or 2022 and remembers the fear viscerally.
Their pain
Whipsawed between two fears: a big drawdown erasing years of gains, and missing out by sitting in cash. They don't have the time horizon to recover from a −50% year.
Your pitch
"You don't need to beat the market — you need to not get destroyed by it, and not panic-sell at the bottom. This kept its worst drawdown near a third of the market's across 20 years, including 2008 and 2022."
Proof to use
The 20-year survivorship-free backtest: ~19% worst drawdown vs the market's ~55%. The systematic discipline — no emotional exits, ever.
Top objection
"It lags in bull markets." → "Yes — by design. That lag is the premium you pay for the crash protection. You're buying a seatbelt, not a faster car."
Disqualify if
They benchmark to the S&P monthly and get itchy about "keeping up." That itch becomes a cancellation the first strong bull quarter.
What closes
Sleep-at-night + honest expectations. "In a raging bull you'll trail, and that's the deal we're making. In the year that scares everyone, you'll be glad you're here."
02  Preserver · the channel play

The Independent RIA / Adviser

Who
Fee-only independent RIA, book of $20–200M. Crucially independent — wirehouse advisers (Merrill, Morgan, etc.) can't approve outside strategies, so they're not your buyer.
Their pain
Career risk is asymmetric: they fear underperforming in a bull market (clients leave) more than participating in a crash (everyone's down together). And they dread the panic-selling call at the bottom.
Your pitch
"Position this as a 10–20% sleeve, not a core holding. It's the part of the book built to hold up when clients are scared — a behavioral tool that keeps them in their seats. Control wins races, not just speed."
Proof to use
Drawdown control + fully systematic (no adviser discretion to defend to a client). Methodology + risk docs for their due diligence. Client-education one-pagers they can hand out.
Top objection
"No live track record." → "Correct — which is exactly why it starts as a small sleeve, and why we're transparent about the live rollout. Start at 5%, watch it prove out, expand."
Disqualify if
Wirehouse (can't allocate), or they want to white-label a "beat-the-market" story. This is a sleeve + a behavioral tool, not a performance headline.
What closes
Land and expand. One small sleeve, transparent reporting, grow with proof. The adviser channel is a multi-year relationship, not a one-call close.
03  Maximizer · new channel

The Disciplined Growth-Seeker

Who
Younger (30–45), higher risk tolerance, growth-minded, follows momentum and tech — but has already been burned by a blow-up (2022, meme stocks, crypto) and knows it.
Their pain
Wants big upside but got wrecked by a drawdown before. Knows, honestly, that they lack the discipline to cut risk at the top — nobody sells into euphoria.
Your pitch
"Offense with a seatbelt. Full momentum upside, but the engine automatically eases off when its own volatility spikes — so you don't ride the next momentum crash to zero. It cut that 2021 crash roughly in half."
Proof to use
Market the durable edge + the crash-brake — never the peak number. The vol-brake is the differentiator vs reckless momentum. Honesty about the aggressive drawdown builds trust here.
Top objection
"Why not just do it myself?" → "Because you won't cut risk at the top — you already know that. This does it for you, systematically, every day."
Disqualify if
They want leverage, options, or lottery tickets. This is disciplined momentum, not a casino. Over-promise here and you've made a churn machine.
What closes
"Upside without the blow-up." Sells as the base Preserver + the Maximizer add-on — offense with the guardrail already installed.
04  Release · do not sell

The Market Maximizer (walk away)

Who
Benchmarks to the S&P obsessively, wants to beat it every quarter, measures success purely in return vs the index.
Why release
This product is an F for them. It will underperform in bull runs (by design), they'll feel it acutely, and they'll churn and badmouth. Every one you sign is a future refund + a bad review.
The graceful exit
"Honestly, this probably isn't the right fit for you — you sound like you want maximum return, and we're built for maximum protection." That line closes the right people in the room and earns respect from the wrong one.

Objection handbook

When they say…You say…
"It lags the market."That's the feature, not the bug. Reframe to drawdown: "You're trading a bit of bull-market upside for a fraction of the crash. That's the whole trade."
"There's no live track record."Be honest — it's true. "The backtest is survivorship-free and point-in-time, and we're transparent about the live rollout. Start small, watch it prove out." Never imply a live record we don't have.

"The fees seem high."Be straight about account size: the drag is ~1.5% at $100k but ~0.6% at $250k and ~0.15% at $1M. "It's built for $250k+ — below that, the math is honestly less compelling."
"How do I know the backtest is real?"This is the due-diligence conversation: survivorship-free, point-in-time, includes companies that later failed. (Use "survivorship-free" here — in due diligence — not as an opening line. It's table stakes, not a headline.)

Qualify in five minutes

Ask these early. The answers sort the prospect into a persona — and tell you when to walk.

  • "What kept you up at night in 2022?" — a real answer = Preserver. A shrug = probably a Maximizer.
  • "How often do you compare your portfolio to the S&P?" — daily/monthly benchmarking = churn risk, disqualify.
  • "Have you ever sold at a bottom and regretted it?" — a yes is the anti-capitulation buying signal.
  • "Is your goal to get rich, or to not get poor?" — the cleanest A/F sort you can ask.
  • "Roughly what are you looking to allocate?" — under $250k, set fee-drag expectations honestly or steer accordingly.

Guardrails — sell honestly or don't sell

Always

  • Lead with the result + thesis (drawdown control), not the methodology.
  • Disclose the bull-market lag up front — it filters and it builds trust.
  • Label every backtest as a backtest.
  • Set the multi-year expectation — this is not a quarter-to-quarter product.
  • Match account size to the $250k+ sweet spot.

Never

  • Never promise beating the market.
  • Never say "guaranteed," "can't lose," or imply a live record we don't yet have.
  • Never lead with "survivorship-free" — it's table stakes, save it for due diligence.
  • Never quote the peak aggressive number — sell the durable edge + the brake.
  • Never sell to a Market Maximizer just to hit a number. It costs more than it makes.
