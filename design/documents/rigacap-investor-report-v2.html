01 · Executive Summary

What RigaCap is, and what we measure.

RigaCap is a disciplined momentum strategy distributed as a subscription publication. Subscribers receive high-conviction signals drawn from a universe of ~4,000 US equities and filtered through a 3-factor entry gauntlet, a market-regime filter, inverse-volatility position sizing, and trailing-stop discipline.

What separates RigaCap from the broader signal-service category is the methodology posture. Every return figure in this document is drawn from walk-forward backtesting — not in-sample optimization, not cherry-picked windows. We publish a single continuous 21-year window, crisis years included, warts included. And the backtesting infrastructure flags data-integrity issues automatically, so the numbers we publish are the ones the strategy actually produced on clean inputs.

RigaCap's live track record began June 11, 2026 with a fresh $100k model book; every figure in this document before that date is backtested and labeled as such. As live execution data accumulates, quarterly disclosure will compare live results to the walk-forward baseline.

Core Result · Continuous 21-Year Walk-Forward (2007–2026, price returns). The strategy compounded at 8.3% annualized (0.73 Sharpe, 0.43 Calmar) with a worst drawdown of 19% — through the 2008 financial crisis, COVID, and the 2022 bear. Over the same window the S&P 500 (price-only) compounded at 9.8% but lost more than half its value twice (55% worst drawdown); raw 12-month momentum compounded at 13.2% with a 57% worst drawdown. The product is not the return — it is the survivability.

Three things to know.

  1. Capital preservation when it matters. In 2008 the regime filter held the portfolio in cash: −0.5% against the S&P 500's −37.7%. In 2022, −7.5% against −19.9%. In 2020, +31.9% against +15.2%. The honest wart: 2018, a whipsaw year, cost −12.3% against the index's −7.0%. We publish that too.
  2. The strategy is holdable. Stress-testing a $100k investor who panic-sells after a −25% drawdown: RigaCap finishes at $473k under every re-entry rule because it never trips the threshold. Raw momentum finishes anywhere between $206k and $1.02M depending on nerve (6–10 panic-sales over the window); the S&P 500 between $210k and $399k. A strategy only compounds for the investor who can stay in it.
  3. The recent held-out window is strong. The last 24 months (June 2024–May 2026) were held out of all tuning: +32.0% annualized, 2.20 Sharpe, 8.5% max drawdown, against +19.9% annualized and a 19.0% drawdown for the S&P 500. We do not project this rate forward; we publish it alongside the 21-year base rate.

03 · Methodology

The Ensemble — three pieces of discipline, all must align.

The system requires three independent factors to align before entering a position. No single indicator is trusted alone. Most days, of the ~4,000 stocks scanned, none pass.

01
Breakout Timing
Price must clear our proprietary long-term accumulation reference by a tested threshold — a signal we've refined over years of indicator work. The entry is either a fresh breakout or it isn't.
02
Momentum Quality
The candidate must rank in the top tier on a composite momentum score: short-term (10-day) and long-term (60-day) returns, weighted, less a volatility penalty. Quality of momentum, not just direction.
03
Breakout Proximity
The candidate must sit within 3% of its 50-day high, drawn from a liquid universe — the top 100 symbols by 60-day volume, with a $15 minimum price and 500k minimum daily volume.
04
Wide Trailing Exit
A 30% trailing stop from the high-water mark — wide enough to hold winners through normal volatility, firm enough to cap any single failure. The exit logic, not the entry, governs how long a position lives.

Portfolio construction.

The system holds up to twenty positions at roughly 4.5% allocation each, sized by inverse volatility so calmer names carry more weight. Diversification is a deliberate choice — the concentrated alternative was tested extensively and produced drawdowns near 30%; spreading the same signals across twenty names is what holds the worst drawdown to 19%.

04 · Cascade Guard

A safeguard we describe precisely, not loosely.

When multiple positions hit their trailing stop on the same day — a rare event signalling cascading market stress — the system pauses all new entries for ten calendar days. This prevents the "buying into a falling knife" pattern where forced re-entries during stress produce concentrated losses.

How it is validated.

Most safeguards in retail signal services are claimed but never quantified. Ours is measured by running the walk-forward backtest a second time with Cascade Guard disabled — a true counterfactual — and comparing the result against the canonical run. A prior strategy configuration showed a meaningful return contribution from the safeguard; the attribution for the current production configuration is being re-measured on the 21-year window and will be published when complete, rather than carried forward from a superseded test.

Cascade Guard has been live in the production scanner since May 2026 and remains enabled in the current production configuration. The strategy stays fully active on the overwhelming majority of trading days; the safeguard only fires on rare cascade-stress events.

What Cascade Guard does NOT do. It is not a drawdown reducer. Existing positions continue to drawdown during paused windows; the safeguard only prevents new capital deployment into stress. Cascade Guard is a re-entry discipline, not a downside hedge — the regime filter is what handles drawdown.

05 · Regime Awareness

Seven regimes detected.
Two responses, by design.

The system classifies market conditions into seven distinct regimes — using SPY trend, VIX percentile, market breadth, new-highs/lows, and momentum acceleration as inputs. Each regime is a real, separable market state.

The seven regimes.

RegimeCharacter
Strong BullSustained breadth, low volatility, narrow drawdowns
Weak BullUp-trend with widening volatility
Rotating BullSector rotation, mixed leadership
Range-BoundNo clear trend, whipsaw risk elevated
Weak BearDown-trend without panic features
Panic / CrashWide drawdowns, breadth collapse, VIX spike
RecoveryPost-stress re-tightening of breadth

The honest scope of the response.

Detection is seven-state. Response is currently binary: the strategy is either invested (market trend healthy) or in cash (market trend broken), with Cascade Guard pausing new entries during cascade-grade stress. Regime-specific sub-strategies (different entry rules per regime, different position sizing per regime, etc.) were evaluated and deferred.

The reason is statistical, not philosophical. Across twenty-one years of data, only a handful of true panic-grade regimes have occurred. That sample size is too small to support per-regime parameter optimization without overfitting. Adding more rules to the system, on this evidence, would degrade the result rather than improve it.

What detection is doing today: providing market context on the dashboard, supporting the binary invested-or-cash decision, and feeding a 1–2 week regime-shift forecast surfaced to subscribers. Per-regime sub-strategies revisit when either (a) more events occur, or (b) a universal rule emerges that doesn't require per-event fitting.

06 · Platform Architecture

Built for durability, not spectacle.

The infrastructure is designed for low operational surface area: small number of moving parts, clear separation between user-facing API and heavyweight compute, and a data pipeline whose failures fail loudly rather than quietly.

Stack summary.

  • API tier. AWS Lambda + API Gateway behind CloudFront with AWS WAF. Stateless, scales horizontally, no single point of failure.
  • Compute tier. Separate Worker Lambda for daily scans, walk-forward simulations, and email generation. Heavy memory footprint isolated from the user-facing path.
  • Data tier. PostgreSQL (RDS) for subscriber and signal state; S3 for historical price data and pre-computed dashboards. A dual-source data pipeline (Alpaca primary, yfinance fallback) with health monitoring and automatic failover.
  • Front end. React + Vite single-page app served via CloudFront.
  • Operational cadence. Daily scan at 4:20 PM ET (post-settlement); daily digest emails at 6 PM ET; intraday alert monitoring during market hours; weekly editorial newsletter on Sundays.

What this means for risk.

The platform has no live brokerage integration. Subscribers receive signals; they execute through their own broker. RigaCap never touches subscriber capital and never has custody of funds. Liability surface is limited to the accuracy and timeliness of the signals themselves.

07 · Risk & Methodology Disclosures

What is — and isn't — modeled.

Every backtest is a partial truth. The honest version of "past performance does not guarantee future results" is a specific list of what the simulation captures and what it does not.

What the simulation includes.

  • Realized end-of-day fills at simulated execution prices
  • A continuous 21-year price history (2007–2026)
  • Survivorship-free, point-in-time data from 2016 onward; the pre-2016 extension carries a survivorship caveat and is disclosed as such wherever 21-year figures are cited
  • Walk-forward methodology with no hindsight bias — parameters fixed, then applied forward into unseen data
  • A fully held-out final 24 months (June 2024–May 2026), untouched by any tuning

What the simulation does NOT include.

  • Slippage modeled at 0%. A subscriber's actual fills will differ by some amount depending on order type, execution venue, and order size. For our universe (price > $15, volume > 500k/day), realistic slippage is typically 0.05–0.20% per round trip.
  • Commissions modeled at $0. Reasonable in the zero-commission era for retail brokers, but worth disclosing.
  • SPY benchmark is price-only (excludes dividends), which understates the index by approximately 1.5–2 percentage points per year.
  • Tax drag. User-specific. A taxable account will pay short- or long-term capital gains; a tax-advantaged account will not. We do not model this; subscribers should.
  • Behavioral drift. The simulation assumes signals are followed exactly. A subscriber who skips trades, sizes them differently, or overrides the trailing stop will diverge from the simulated result.

Material disclosures.

RigaCap operates as a publisher under the publisher’s exemption to the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 (§202(a)(11)(d)), as interpreted in Lowe v. SEC. Three conditions are satisfied: the commentary is impersonal (the same signals go to every subscriber, regardless of their portfolio or financial situation); it is bona fide market analysis; and it is published on a regular schedule. Subscribers execute their own trades through their own broker. Past performance does not predict future results, and the strategy may materially underperform any single benchmark in any given period.

08 · Growth Roadmap

Specific commitments. No hand-waving.

The growth plan is constrained by two non-negotiables: subscriber capacity at the strategy level (~1,500 before collective market impact becomes material), and operational capacity at the founder-led level (~800–1,500 with automation). Within those, the path is straightforward.

Near-term (next 90 days).

  • Editorial cadence build-out. Sunday newsletter ("The Market, Measured") and Tuesday regime report establish the editorial publication posture. Topic backlog of ~20 educational pieces in pipeline.
  • Production parity discipline. Cascade Guard is now live in the production scanner (May 2026), closing the final WF↔production parity gap. Any future backtest improvement ports to production in lockstep so subscribers can realize what we publish.

Medium-term (3–9 months).

  • Subscriber growth to ~500 paid. Acquisition through editorial content, AI-driven social proof from real trade results, and methodology transparency — not paid advertising.
  • Storage migration to Parquet. The pickle-based historical data store is durable through ~500 paid subscribers; beyond that, a Parquet-on-S3 migration improves cold-start latency and reduces operational risk.
  • Live track record vs. simulation. By the 12-month mark, a meaningful body of live execution data accumulates. Quarterly disclosure comparing live results to simulation becomes a differentiating editorial asset.

Longer-term (12+ months).

  • Pricing discipline. No price reductions before 12 months of live track record. Founding-member pricing ($59/mo) locked for the first 100 subscribers; standard pricing ($129/mo) and annual ($1,099) hold.
  • Capacity ceiling. Approaching 1,000–1,500 active subscribers, the operational response is to raise prices to ration demand, not to chase growth past the strategy's natural capacity.
  • Regulatory readiness. Securities counsel engaged; Marketing Rule compliance reviewed quarterly; registration as an investment advisor revisits at the appropriate scale.
Build a publication that pays for itself, an audience that compounds, and a track record that doesn't require explanation.
