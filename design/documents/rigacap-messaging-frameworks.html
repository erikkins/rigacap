The Core Insight

Every competitor in our space says some version of the same thing: "AI-powered stock analysis tools for smarter trading." They compete on features — more screeners, more indicators, more customization.

RigaCap doesn't work that way.

Users don't build strategies. They don't pick tickers. They don't configure parameters. They subscribe, open their dashboard, and see exactly which stocks to buy and when to sell. The system does the work. The user makes the call.

This is the gap between what we are and how we currently talk about ourselves. The word "signals" is accurate but generic. "AI-powered" is table stakes. We need language that communicates the real difference: you don't use RigaCap — you follow it.

What RigaCap IS

  • A curated signal service with a proven track record
  • A membership — subscribe and receive
  • A system that does the analysis for you
  • Opinionated: we tell you what we'd buy
  • Simple: open, read, decide, act via your broker

What RigaCap is NOT

  • A stock screener or research tool
  • A platform you "use" or configure
  • A DIY toolkit for building strategies
  • Neutral: we don't show you both sides
  • A brokerage — we never touch your money

The question this document tries to answer: If someone asks "What is RigaCap?" — what do we want the answer to be?

Honest Critique

What works: "Beat the Market" is bold and clear. The 3-factor structure gives specificity. Performance stats are strong proof points.

What doesn't: "AI-Powered Trading Signals" is generic — it describes 200+ competitors. "Ensemble approach" is jargon most people won't understand. The copy emphasizes how the system works (timing, momentum, risk) rather than what the user gets (curated buy/sell decisions). "Join thousands of traders" implies we're a tool for traders, not a signal service for busy people.

Risk: This positioning invites feature-by-feature comparison with cheaper tools. It attracts power users who want to tinker, not subscribers who want answers.
