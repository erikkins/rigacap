Hi there,

I want to be straight with you about a couple of changes I just made to RigaCap — including one that's going to sound backwards at first. You're here early, so you get the honest version.

First, the uncomfortable part. When I went back and stress-tested our strategy on cleaner, more honest data, I found our old numbers had been flattering us. They quietly left out companies that went bankrupt, and a couple of data quirks had inflated the returns. So I rebuilt the whole thing from scratch — and revised our numbers down. I'd rather show you a smaller number I can stand behind than a big one I can't.

Now the part that sounds backwards. I also deliberately changed the strategy to chase less return — and more safety. On paper that looks like a downgrade. Let me explain why it's actually the opposite, because this is the most important thing I'll tell you all year.

There's a number that matters far more than the headline return, and almost nobody talks about it: the drawdown. It's simply how far your account falls from its highest point before it recovers.

The old, flashier version of our strategy had a 35% drawdown. In plain terms: at some point, you'd watch a $100,000 account fall to $65,000. And here's the problem — almost nobody can sit through that. People panic and sell at the very bottom, locking in the loss… right before the recovery they'd been waiting for. So that "high return" only ever existed on paper, for someone who never actually lived through the scary part.

So I cut the worst-case drop roughly in half — down to about 17%. A 17% dip is something you can actually stomach, and hold through.

Because the best strategy isn't the one with the biggest number on a chart. It's the one you can actually stay invested in. A 14% return you keep beats a 22% return you bail on — every single time.

It's a bit like a car. One that does 200 mph but terrifies you past 60 is worse than one that does 120 you'll actually floor. The returns that reach your account are only the ones you can hold through.

One last bit of honesty. These are still backtested results — a rigorous simulation, not a live track record yet. The live clock is starting right now, and I'll keep showing you the real results as they come in. Good or bad. That's the deal.

Thank you for being here early. You're not just testing a product — you're keeping me honest. That's worth more than you know.

— Erik
