to be his own harshest critic.

No team to impress, no fund to raise, no incentive to oversell. Just the discipline to catch my own mistakes before you ever see them.

Erik Kins
Founder, RigaCap

I'm Erik Kins — a self-taught engineer and former Chief Innovation Officer. For fifteen years I ran quantitative research as a parallel practice alongside a career building software. RigaCap is what happens when those tracks converge: an institution-grade research process, run by one person, priced for individuals.

Working solo cuts both ways, and I'd rather be honest about it. The upside is there's no marketing department to spin you and no investors to placate. The downside is that I'm the only one who can catch my own errors — so the entire discipline of this operation is built around doing exactly that.

The clearest example is recent.

When I rebuilt my research data foundation to be survivorship-free — to include the companies that delisted, merged, and went bankrupt, not just today's survivors — and made every price decision strictly point-in-time, my honest numbers came in more conservative than my earlier figures. So I revised them down. The strategy I put my name on is the one that survived me trying my hardest to break it.

"The number I publish is the one that's left after I've done everything I can to disprove it."

That's the whole philosophy. RigaCap isn't a lottery ticket dressed up as an algorithm. It's a risk-managed implementation of a real, well-documented market anomaly — momentum — harvested with enough discipline to roughly halve its worst-case drawdown. Backtested rigorously, now running live, and I’ll publish the real-time results as they accrue. Good or bad.
