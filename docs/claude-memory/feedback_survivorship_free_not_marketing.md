Erik (Jun 29 2026): "survivorship-free is table stakes and should not be used in marketing lingo."

**Why:** survivorship-free backtesting is the bare minimum for any honest quant result — touting it to customers reads like bragging about doing the obvious. It's load-bearing *internally* (it's WHY our numbers are trustworthy, and it's how our two-step rigor caught every in-sample mirage), but it's not a differentiator worth marketing.

**How to apply:** keep "survivorship-free" out of customer-facing copy (landing, newsletter, decks). Internally, keep using it as the validation standard. When framing the product's edge, lead with the *result* and the *thesis* (e.g., modern-market momentum, drawdown discipline — see [[project_alpha_maximizer_sleeve_idea]]), not the methodology hygiene.
