## ⭐ THE strategic insight (reshapes adviser positioning)
**Career-risk asymmetry:** "We're more likely to get fired for underperforming a strong bull market than for participating in a major decline." Advisers fear lagging the bull (years between crashes) MORE than the crash. So the adviser's real question isn't "is it good?" but "will this cost me clients during the long bull stretches?" → **RESOLUTION = position as a SLEEVE (10-20% of a broader allocation), NOT a standalone portfolio.** Paul handed this himself ("could cover a sleeve within a broader allocation"). The sleeve makes the career math work: doesn't tank book-level relative perf in a bull, delivers the drawdown cushion + behavioral story when it matters. **/for-advisers should pivot from "a strategy" → "a risk-managed sleeve that keeps your clients invested."**

## Channel reality
- **Wirehouses (RJ / Merrill / Morgan Stanley) WON'T approve** — heavy compliance, must fit "in a box, defendable, repeatable," usually platform-standardized. RJ is OUT.
